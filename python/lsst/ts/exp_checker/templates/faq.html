FAQ

This FAQ collects answers to a few commonly occuring questions. More support can be found on the {{ slack_channel }} channel. For technical requests (bug reports or improvements), open a GitHub issue.

What do the mask colors mean?

The LSST Science Pipelines store information about why a pixel is masked in a "bit mask plane", and we color the mask based on the highest bit in this plane. We try to follow the Science Pipelines convention for mapping colors to mask bits, which can be found here.

What happens with my submissions?

Your submissions are recorded each time you click the button. It's not a problem if you missed something on the image or you hit the button accidentially, since we don't have to be complete in finding problems. We do seek to be reliable when we state that something is problematic.

False positives vs false negatives?

We would like to know about any kind of flaw in the data. Therefore, please mark both false negative and false positive instances of artifacts (e.g., use "Edge Bleed" to mark both an edge bleed without an edge bleed mask and a edge bleed mask without an edge bleed). We provide a button, that allows you to report a false-positive treatment of such a problem.

What's up with the underlined letters on buttons?

Any underlined letter on a button indicates that you can use a keyboard shortcut. In the two cases below, you can use Alt+m to toggle the mask and Alt+s to toggle the scaling. Hint for Mac users: Alt = Option

Why are some of the bleed trails one-sided?

Bleed trails result when a bright object saturates one or more pixels and charge overflows along the readout direction. There is a barrier between the top and bottom of the e2v sensors that stops charge from flowing between the two halves of the CCD. This causes some of the bleed trails to appear asymmetric depending on their position in the channel and the amount of charge overflow.

I can't see the images. What's wrong?

Very likely your browser doesn't support the way we load the images. Safari version 5 is an example for this lack of implementation of a nominally supported functionality. In this case, please use another/newer browser instead.
